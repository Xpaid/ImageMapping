Rizal Province

Nestled east of Metro Manila, Rizal province in the Philippines pulsates with a captivating mix of urban energy and natural beauty. Named after national hero Dr. Jose Rizal, the province boasts bustling cities like Antipolo, famed for its pilgrimage site, the Shrine of Our Lady of Antipolo. While portions of Rizal, like Pasig and Taguig, have become part of Metro Manila and house prominent business districts, the province itself offers escapes into nature. Hikers and trekkers can explore the Sierra Madre mountain range, while Laguna de Bay, the country's largest lake, graces the southern border with its scenic beauty. Rizal's historical significance extends beyond its namesake. Fort Bonifacio, a pivotal military camp during the Philippine Revolution, stands within its borders, alongside museums and ancestral houses whispering tales of the past. A vibrant cultural tapestry unfolds through diverse festivals, local cuisine, and historical landmarks, making Rizal a captivating destination easily accessible for exploration from nearby Metro Manila.
